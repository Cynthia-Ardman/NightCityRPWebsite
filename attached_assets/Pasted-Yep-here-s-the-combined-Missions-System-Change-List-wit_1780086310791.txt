## 3. Mission Statuses

Mission status should use exactly these values:

1. Open
2. Pending
3. Completed
4. Completed, Players Paid
5. Completed, Paid
6. Cancelled

---

## 4. Mission Status Definitions

### Open

**Meaning:** Actively selecting players.

Use this when the mission has been created and the fixer is still choosing/selecting players.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Mission can be visible as Open while player selection is still happening.
* Open missions should not count as attended.
* Open missions should not trigger payment.
* Open missions may still be edited, assigned, rescheduled, or cancelled by fixers/admins.

---

### Pending

**Meaning:** Players selected, waiting for mission to be run.

Use this once players/characters have been selected and the mission is scheduled but has not happened yet.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Pending missions appear on selected players’ dashboards.
* Pending missions show date/time in the viewer’s local time.
* Pending missions should not pay anyone until after the mission has occurred.
* Pending missions may still be rescheduled or cancelled by fixers/admins.

---

### Completed

**Meaning:** Mission has finished.

Use this when the mission is over, but payments have not been completed yet.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Completed missions indicate the mission happened.
* Attendance may be recorded once the mission is completed.
* Payment state should still show as incomplete.
* Fixers/admins should be able to see that payment still needs to happen.

---

### Completed, Players Paid

**Meaning:** Mission is over and player characters have been paid, but actors have not been paid yet.

This status should include a clear reminder/warning to fixers that actors still need to be paid.

**Fixer reminder text example**

`Players have been paid. Actors have not been paid yet.`

**Acceptance criteria**

* Status exists separately from fully paid.
* Fixers see an obvious reminder that actor payments are still outstanding.
* Mission should not be considered fully paid while actor payments are missing.
* Actor payment tools should remain visible/available to fixers/admins.

---

### Completed, Paid

**Meaning:** Mission is over and everyone has been paid.

This is the final successful status after both players and actors have been paid.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Mission only reaches this status once all required player and actor payments are complete.
* No outstanding payment reminders appear after this status.
* Mission should be treated as closed/fully resolved.
* Payment tools should not allow accidental duplicate payment.

---

### Cancelled

**Meaning:** Mission has been cancelled.

If a mission is cancelled:

* No one gets paid.
* No attendance is recorded.
* Any scheduled auto-pay should be disabled.
* Any scheduled attendance credit should be disabled.
* Discord event should be cancelled or updated to show cancellation if supported.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Cancelled missions do not generate attendance records.
* Cancelled missions do not trigger player payments.
* Cancelled missions do not trigger actor payments.
* Cancelled missions are excluded from mission attendance counts.
* Discord event reflects cancellation where possible.
* Cancelled missions remain visible for audit/history unless intentionally hidden by filters.

---

## 6. Status Transition Logic

### Expected Flow

Typical mission lifecycle:

`Open → Pending → Completed → Completed, Players Paid → Completed, Paid`

Alternative flow:

`Open/Pending → Cancelled`

**Acceptance criteria**

* Mission statuses follow the approved lifecycle.
* Cancelled can happen before completion.
* Completed is used when the mission happened but payments are not yet complete.
* Completed, Players Paid is used when player payments are complete but actor payments are still incomplete.
* Completed, Paid is only used when everyone has been paid.

## 22. Auto-Pay Players After Mission

After the mission starts, wait approximately 3–4 hours before auto-paying assigned players.

Auto-pay should only happen if:

* Mission was not cancelled.
* Mission was not rescheduled to a future time.
* Player is still assigned to the mission.
* Player has an assigned character.
* Mission has not already paid that player.

Auto-pay each assigned player the stated mission pay amount.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Players are paid automatically after the mission window.
* Cancelled missions do not auto-pay.
* Rescheduled future missions do not auto-pay early.
* Players are not double-paid.
* Mission payment state updates correctly after payment.

## 27. Payment Completion Logic

Player payments and actor payments must be tracked separately.

### Player Payments

When assigned players are paid:

* Record payment.
* Post payment log to the banking channel.
* Update mission status appropriately.

If players are paid but actors are not:

* Set status to `Completed, Players Paid`.
* Show fixer reminder that actor payments are still needed.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Player payments are tracked separately from actor payments.
* Player payments are tied to the mission, player, and selected character.
* Player payments are logged to `#banking`.
* Player payment records prevent duplicate payment for the same mission/player pair.

---

### Actor Payments

When actors are paid:

* Record payment.
* Post payment notice to `#npc-spending`.
* Post/log payment to the banking channel where appropriate.
* If players and actors are both fully paid, set status to `Completed, Paid`.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Actor payments are tracked separately from player payments.
* Actor payments are tied to the mission and actor/user.
* Actor payments are posted to `#npc-spending`.
* Actor payments are logged to `#banking` where appropriate.
* Actor payment records prevent duplicate payment for the same mission/actor pair.

## 34. Prevent Double Payment

The system must prevent accidental double payment.

**Required safeguards**

* Do not allow the same player to be paid twice for the same mission as a player.
* Do not allow the same actor to be paid twice for the same mission as an actor.
* Manual payment and auto-payment should check existing records before paying.
* Failed payment attempts should not mark someone as paid.
* Partial payment failure should leave the mission in the correct incomplete payment status.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Re-running payment logic does not duplicate payments.
* Staff can see who has and has not been paid.
* Mission status accurately reflects payment completion.
* Payment errors are visible to fixers/admins for follow-up.
