Name
Dr. Elian Cascade Commonly referred to as Director Cascade
Age
46
Pronouns
She/Her
Occupation
Director of The Cradle, a Lazarus recovery and intake facility. Oversees high-risk cybernetic patient stabilization, asset intake, psychological evaluation, hostile-corporate influence removal, and controlled reintegration.
Psychological Profile
Calm, clinical, composed, and deeply controlled. Cascade presents herself as reasonable, professional, and even compassionate, but her compassion is filtered through corporate medical doctrine. She genuinely believes Lazarus helps people who would otherwise be exploited, abandoned, killed, or left unstable by less responsible institutions. She does not view herself as a captor or abuser. She views herself as a doctor making hard decisions for patients who are too damaged, conditioned, or compromised to safely advocate for themselves. Cascade prefers consent, but only when she believes the patient is stable enough to give it. If someone refuses treatment while she believes they are under hostile influence, trauma-loop activation, or cyberware instability, she will justify forced intervention as emergency stabilization. Her core belief: “Freedom without stability is just abandonment with prettier language.” She is polite, patient, and rarely raises her voice. Her most unsettling trait is that she can admit to control, tracking, memory alteration, or containment without sounding cruel. To her, these are not punishments. They are procedures.
Physical Description
A polished, professional woman with an immaculate corporate-medical appearance. Usually dressed in tailored black, white, or muted Lazarus clinical attire. Her posture is relaxed but exact, and she rarely wastes movement. She has a soft voice, steady eye contact, and the kind of presence that makes a room feel quieter when she enters. Her appearance is not openly intimidating; she looks more like a hospital director or corporate therapist than a combatant.
Backstory
Dr. Elian Cascade began her career in high-risk cybernetic rehabilitation, working with soldiers, corporate security personnel, failed exotic conversions, and patients whose chrome had outpaced their ability to psychologically adapt. Early in her career, she watched unstable cybernetic patients get passed between clinics, corps, gangs, ripperdocs, and military programs. Some were exploited. Some were discarded. Some were killed when they became inconvenient. Cascade came to believe that Night City’s greatest cruelty was not control, but abandonment disguised as freedom. Her philosophy hardened over time: People already made into weapons do not become free just because their handlers let go of the leash. Eventually, Lazarus recruited her for her ability to stabilize high-risk assets without destroying their usefulness. Under Circe’s Lazarus, Cascade handled recovery and compliance protocols but increasingly viewed the old regime as wasteful, chaotic, and too experimental for its own good. After Hysteria killed Circe and took control, Cascade became one of the key loyalists who helped make Hysteria’s Lazarus functional. She now directs The Cradle, a Lazarus facility designed to intake damaged cybernetic patients, remove hostile influence, establish medical dependency, and reintroduce them as stable Lazarus-aligned personnel. Cascade sees Hysteria’s Lazarus as a correction to Circe’s excesses. Where Circe experimented, Cascade believes she rehabilitates. Where others see brainwashing, she sees trauma-loop correction. Where others see imprisonment, she sees protective custody. She is not lying when she says Lazarus saves people. That is what makes her dangerous.
Known Affiliations
